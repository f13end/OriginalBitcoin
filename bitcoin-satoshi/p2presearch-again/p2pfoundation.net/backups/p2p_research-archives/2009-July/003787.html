>From the Participatory Culture Foundation...

Yes! We have just released Miro 2.5, a major update with new features,
faster performance, audio podcasts, and lots of polish.

Download Miro 2.5
<http://getmiro.com/>

*Here are the biggest changes:*

• Miro 2.5 launches 2 – 4 times faster (especially for users with lots of
videos and feeds)

• New audio podcast section of the Miro Guide

• Better interface for audio playback

• Button to download from YouTube when you add as sidebar site – also see: <
http://www.getmiro.com/blog/2009/07/4-ways-to-download-media-files-including-torrents-within-miro/
>

• Smarter handling of bittorrent files, especially videos

• New Library interface with ‘Video’, ‘Audio’, and ‘Other’ sections

• New keyboard shortcuts for virtually all functions (great for programming
a remote control)

• Interface polish and refinement

• Easier hacking: the database layer was rewritten to use a simplified
SQLite schema and we’ve created a new developer documentation site. <
http://pculture.org/wguaraldi/miro/2.5/developer/> (more deets <
http://bluesock.org/~willg/blog/miro/developer_docs.html>)

• Lots of bug fixes and tweaks

For a more complete list of what’s new and different, see the Miro 2.5
Release Notes. <
https://develop.participatoryculture.org/trac/democracy/wiki/2.5ReleaseNotes
>

Please help us spread the word, tweet about Miro 2.5. <http://bit.ly/jHNbb>
