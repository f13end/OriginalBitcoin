 Since its announcement last month, the Supernova
Hub<http://www.supernovahub.com/conversation>has posted a plethora of
insights on Change
in the Network Age<http://supernovahub.com/2009/06/welcome-post-change-is-good/>.
(Please see sidebar for available video interviews with thought-leaders.)

This month, we're announcing a new weekly teleconference series -- *Supernova
NetworkAge Briefings*<http://supernovahub.com/conversation/network-age-briefings/>.
On these free, open calls, frank discussion is encouraged (and civil
disagreement is embraced). As a follow up to his recent
interviews<http://supernovahub.com/2009/07/clay-shirky-andrew-keen-and-the-real-time-web/>with
Clay
Shirky <http://supernovahub.com/2009/07/supernova-interview-clay-shirky/>and
Andrew
Keen <http://supernovahub.com/2009/07/supernova-interview-andrew-keen/>,
Howard Greenstein will moderate the first one-hour Briefing on *Thursday,
August 6, 11am PST/2pm EST*, featuring Andrew Keen ("The Cult of the
Amateur") and Erick Schonfeld (Co-Editor,
TechCrunch<http://www.techcrunch.com/>).


*The topic:* *The "Real Time Web" has worsened information overload. Many
agree that information flowing across our news, email, Facebook, Twitter and
other flows needs to be curated or filtered. But, who should decide 'All The
News That's Fit To Share?'*

We'll live-stream Briefings from our
website<http://supernovahub.com/conversation/network-age-briefings/>as
well as on the BlogTalkRadio
Supernova channel <http://www.blogtalkradio.com/Supernova> with live chat
and moderated Q&A. For more details on how to participate, please go
to the information
page <http://supernovahub.com/conversation/network-age-briefings/>. You may
also sign up <http://supernovahub.com/signup/> to receive regular
notifications on scheduled Briefings, topics and participants.

*Mix it up with John Hagel -- Wharton | SF, August 11th*

We're delighted to launch our first 2009 Supernova Mixer event at Wharton |
SF Campus on *Tuesday, August 11, 5:30pm - 7:30pm*. We invite you to join a
lively conversation with Deloitte's <http://bit.ly/b4M7K> John Hagel and
John Seely Brown on *The Shift
Index*<http://www.deloitte.com/dtt/cda/doc/content/us_tmt_ce_ShiftIndex_0620092_1344%283%29.pdf>--
a major new effort to track the real impacts of what we call the
Network
Age. In their initial report, they address why: most companies fail to
harness "Real Time Web" connected knowledge flows for competitive advantage
• Return on Assets of US firms is falling even as productivity
increases •the gap between winners and losers in business keeps
growing
• and, big companies are losing their leadership positions faster than ever.


To attend this free event and network with the Supernova community, please sign
up <http://supernovasfmixer09.eventbrite.com/>.
