Aren't transitions important?  Even with the sort of collapse seen post WW2,
basic systems deployed along lines then existing.  Do you anticipate a sort
of collapse that requires a walk-off from existing frameworks?  I personally
doubt that will happen soon.  I have no moral disinclination to makeovers,
do overs, revolutions, etc., but I do have moral disinclination to
re-education programs that are coercive, restricting, top-down.  It seems to
me that Stalin-ism, Castro-ism, Leninism were/are even bigger failures that
Victorian capitalism or investment bank capitalism.  Post-modernism is an
intriguing term, but it must mean something concrete.

So, you either have to envision a collapse of end-times proportions, or you
have to look for niche ecological (evolution versus revolution) approaches.


The "what" for me...the performance games, are modes of offering certain
types of competitive advantages where like-minded policy groups (networks)
want to live, act, perform in a certain way...more sustainably, for
instance.

P2P seems an intermediate ground between individualism and coercive
collectivism.  It is structured individualism.  Regulatory capitalism (New
Deal Social Democracy) seems to me to be slightly coercive collectivism.
Maybe that's an inaccurate continuum, but it is how I have envisioned it.  I
see P2P as a framework of determining what by using technocracy to design
open, sustainable models that rely on little hierachy.  P2P reduces the
state, but keeps regulatory frameworks.


Ryan Lanham
