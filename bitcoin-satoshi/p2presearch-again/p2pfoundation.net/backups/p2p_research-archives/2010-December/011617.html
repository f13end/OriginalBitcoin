> Dante,
>
> Lots of questions.  Don't have all of the answers yet.  Fortunately, this
> is an organic process and the answers will emerge when needed.
>
> Regardless, here's a first pass (with the caveat that all of this is likely
> to be revised as the effort progresses and circumstances dictate):
>
> 1) Partnerships?  Sure.  However, this effort is focused on building
> ventures as opposed to middleware.
>
> 2) License for the code?  Hasn't been determined yet.  However, this
> project's objective isn't merely a release of code/methods in the global
> mix.  It's the release code/methods in a way that produces the positive
> feedback loops necessary to produce much, much more of it (required for
> self-organizing systems).
>
> 3) Ownership of physical property?  There aren't any set rules for how the
> ownership of physical property is handled within those ventures that require
> it.  I suspect lots of options will be available in that regard.  Remember,
> this is a decentralized system, which means there will be lots of
> options/method of ownership to choose from (this allows us lots of
> experimentation and the opportunity to avoid brittle ideological rule sets
> developed a priori).
>
> 4) Sweat equity?  A better way to put it: meaningful contributions generate
> an ongoing call on the output of the venture.  Philosophically, the online
> venture is just a tool, a means of producing something of value, and is not
> an entity in its own right.
>
> Sincerely,
>
> JR

>>
>>
>> Here's a quick status update.
>>
>> Spent the last couple of weeks working on a business concept that fully
>> leverages the open source venture concept.  Found one.
>>
>> The next step is to turn the concept into a living open source venture.
>>
>> To accomplish this, we're in the process of setting up a wiki to flesh out
>> the venture's elements. Should be live early in January.
>>
>> It's very likely that we are going to form this venture as a non-profit.
>>  This means that nobody is going to get rich building it.
>>
>> However, everyone that makes a meaningful contribution to he venture prior
>> to launch has a chance at becoming a founder of the venture. All founders
>> get equal compensation from the venture if it is successful.
>>
>> The vast majority of the income generated by the venture will be allocated
>> to the participants (including founders, although through a different
>> calculation) depending on contribution.  Again, nobody is going to get rich
>> building this venture, but it will help you cover your bills if you make a
>> meaningful contribution.
>>
>> This is going to be fun.  It may also become the most rewarding, on
>> numerous levels, project you will ever get the chance to work on.
