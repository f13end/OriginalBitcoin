> A few quick notes ...
>
> We usually think of community owned connectivity as primarily a technical
> challenge. And while the technical challenges are real, there are equally
> significant political, legal, business, financing, marketing, education, and
> design challenges ahead of us. The connective touches virtually every aspect
> of our society and is going to need wisdom and experience from many
> different fields.
>
> So please don't feel that you need to have technical expertise to get
> involved. If you share a sense of urgency in protecting the shape of our
> emerging network culture, and you see an intersection between the connective
> and your own work (and play) then, please, jump in.
>
> In fact, I've started reaching out to the permaculture community  - a
> movement with very little, if any, involvement in digital technologies; But
> they have plenty of insights and wisdom to share on empowering people to
> design resilient, self-sufficient systems in their own local environments
> ... which cuts straight to the core of the connective. A citizen owned
> internet can't be built like a highway ... it has to grow from the inside
> out.
>
