Given all that has been said, especially by our two IP experts Carolina and
Andrew,

it seems to me we have to retain NC, Patrick's suggestion notwithstanding,

however, we could add some notice that :

1) if authors write their own original material, they could authorise
commercial use, but they should ask an explicit notice on their individual
entry?

I think this way we can satisfy people like Patrick who care about the NC
restriction, as long as they take responsiblity for their own material,

Michel

> It depends on:
>
> 1. The law of a given country
>
> 2. The way that fair use is employed (thorough referencing is best to
> make the case for fair use)
>
> 3. The purpose of the wiki in which content is re-used under "Fair use"
>
> For instance, if p2p wiki itself is considered to be primarily for
> non-commercial, educational and informative purposes, and content is
> added from elsewhere under "Fair use", then it might actually make
> sense to use a license that is BY-NC-SA for "release" of the
> combination of original and sourced content (thus at least making an
> effort to ensure your relaying of content is meant for informational
> purposes).
>
> I'd to know what others in the p2presearch community think about the
> case of p2pwiki and it's license. Knowing that possibly the majority
> of content is re-posted from other sources without explicit
> permission, under "Fair Use" assumption, it might just be best to
> retain the BY-NC-SA so that p2pwiki can show it has done everything it
> can to ensure re-used content remains in a "Fair Use" context should
> it be re-used again outside of p2wiki?
>
>
> > now again, feel free to change the license in any way you like, i just
> want
> > this point to be clear,

> >> >
> >> > If you wish to use copyrighted material from this site for purposes of

> >> >
> >> > This means that our Creative Commons rule only applies to material
> that
> >> > is
> >> > originally written for our site, and only concerns a limited amount of
> >> > documents.
> >> >
> >> > [edit] Some practical recommendations
> >> >
> >> > Please note there are two kinds of pages here.
> >> >
> >> > 1) The "impersonal ones", that are continuous upgrades of third party
> >> > material and citations, through a process called opportunistic
> updating,
> >> > which is in agreement with Fair Use or 'fair dealing' (Canada)
> >> > principles.
> >> >
> >> > In such pages, it is not recommended to copy the full article, only
> the
> >> > paragraph that is relevant to the topic at hand. These original quotes
> >> > should not be modified, and should always be sourced. If you add any
> >> > comments, then such a personal voice should be stated as such. It is
> >> > recommended to use the talk pages first in case of disagreement.
> >> >
> >> > 2) personal project pages, where individuals describe their own
> concepts
> >> > and
> >> > projects. Also in this case, respect the individual voice of the
> author,
> >> > but
> >> > these pages should be clearly stated as such.
