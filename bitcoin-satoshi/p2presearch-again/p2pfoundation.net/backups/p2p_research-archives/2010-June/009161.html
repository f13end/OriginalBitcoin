It depends on:

1. The law of a given country

2. The way that fair use is employed (thorough referencing is best to
make the case for fair use)

3. The purpose of the wiki in which content is re-used under "Fair use"

For instance, if p2p wiki itself is considered to be primarily for
non-commercial, educational and informative purposes, and content is
added from elsewhere under "Fair use", then it might actually make
sense to use a license that is BY-NC-SA for "release" of the
combination of original and sourced content (thus at least making an
effort to ensure your relaying of content is meant for informational
purposes).

I'd to know what others in the p2presearch community think about the
case of p2pwiki and it's license. Knowing that possibly the majority
of content is re-posted from other sources without explicit
permission, under "Fair Use" assumption, it might just be best to
retain the BY-NC-SA so that p2pwiki can show it has done everything it
can to ensure re-used content remains in a "Fair Use" context should
it be re-used again outside of p2wiki?
