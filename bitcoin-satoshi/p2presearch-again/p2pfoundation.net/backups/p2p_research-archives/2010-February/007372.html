> Well, sure.  But abolishing copyright law, which has been the main
> source of contention here, is itself an enormous "if" with little
> short term chance of being adopted at all.
>
> So in advocating for such an enormous and unlikely change, I may as
> well go whole hog and discuss the broader elimination of all forms of

> property would exacerbate inconviences resulting from the abolition of

>
> And in fact, I doubt the laws will ever be formally removed until
> they're a moot point anyway.  My main interest is in promoting the
> forms of technological development and counter-organization that will
> render all forms of artificial scarcity unenforceable.  And in that
> regard, I think the unenforceability of one form of artificial
> scarcity will have synergistic effects in making the unenforceability
> of others more beneficial.
