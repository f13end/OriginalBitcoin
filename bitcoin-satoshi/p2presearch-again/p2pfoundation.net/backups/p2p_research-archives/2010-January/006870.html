I agree that his frustration with that is justified, but I think it
falls under my original statement that his critique applied mainly to
very small specialty areas.

And his beef with Wikipedia's treatment of alternative medicine
doesn't jibe very well with the general language he used to criticize
Wikipedia's editing practices.

When most people hear a complaint that Wikipedia lets crazy vagrants
edit it from library computers, they're not thinking "Oh, yeah, those
damn shopping bag people keep editing out the alternative medicine
material and replacing it with mainstream medical material."

The press is full of criticisms of "the cult of the amateur" that
sound very much like Bolen's, but most such people object to what they
see as excess freedom for advocates of the alternative (whether it be
alt medicine, heterodox econ, or whatever) without being properly
subject to gatekeeping authority by properly qualified
"professionals."
