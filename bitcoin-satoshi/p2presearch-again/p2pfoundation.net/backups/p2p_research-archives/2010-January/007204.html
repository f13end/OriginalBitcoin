> distinguishing what uses they want to allow.  It gives far too much power to

This is the framework for the 21st century defined in a few words.  I
disagree with your views.  But the statement is very much correct as to the
point of debate.

To me, the only hope is autonomous machines.  We are rapidly headed there.
The intermediate step is machines controlled by regimes from afar--DRM is a
logical enabler of that.  I see nothing evolving which isn't predictable in
terms of technology.  It is the social/political systems evolution which are
unpredictable.  That's because we don't have good modern theory.  We keep
going backward looking for answers.  If we had hundreds of Romer's...the
real scarcity, we'd have options.  All the Marx's, Smith's, Jefferson's and
Keynes's have little to say to us about were we are headed.  That's because
it is technology that defines society, not vice versa.
