*Reality Sandwich's Featured Contributor: Antonio
Lopez<http://www.realitysandwich.com/sites/realitysandwich.civicactions.net/modules/civicrm/extern/url.php?u=2359&qid=76599>
*

Antonio is a media critic, author and educator with roots in the Los
Angeles' hardcore punk scene in the early '80s. He co-founded a zine called
*Ink Disease* and has written for *Mondo 2000*, *High Times*, *Punk Planet*,
and *In These Times*. He's maintained a spiritual and ecological
orientation, leading to his involvement with Dharma Punx, Buddhism,
bioregionalism, permaculture, and a media literacy educator. His book
*Mediacology:
A Multicultural Approach to Media Literacy in the 21st Century* ties
together these different influences and life themes. He's currently working
on a PhD in education and sustainability while teaching, blogging and living
in Rome, Italy.

RS: What brought you to Reality Sandwich?

A. I met Daniel at a party (just kidding). Actually, it was a little more
fortuitous. I'm a friend of RS contributor Erik Davis who connected me with
Daniel and the original print version of RS (when it was Evolver), for which
I wrote a piece. When Daniel and I finally hooked up, a bunch of us had a
"meeting of the mind's eye" as we spent a night cruising art events and
readings in Brooklyn (along with Propaganda Anonymous) while connecting
deeply around the potential relationship between pre-Columbian philosophy
and modern media. The initial dialog continued when RS was launched. What
motivates me to continue contributing to RS is the opportunity to be
involved in a community of lively minds and open hearts, where it's safe to
talk about all the strange things we are normally forbidden to discuss (such
as in my current role as a "professor"). Doing RS reminds me of the energy
we felt in the old punk days when we made it up as we went, and enjoyed
experimenting with the communication tools we had at the time (Xerox
machine, typewriter, glue, scissors-- no PCs back then!). Now we have a big
new toy to play with called the Internet, and I think it's incredibly fun
and dynamic to filter, link, connect, chat, comment and dialog
instantaneously with an intelligent, like-minded tribe of freaks. It's hard
to come by, so I offer gratitude.

RS: What transformations do you hope to see in your lifetime?

A. Basically I expect the last 5,000 year-cycle of civilization to end. What
I mean by that is not death and destruction (because that's what we *will*
have if civilization does not evolve), nor the end of time in the scary
sense that suddenly we'll be sucked into a black hole or destroyed by evil
aliens. I think we will go back to time as art, and reconnect with nature,
which has been veiled from our perception because of the various historical
trends we are well familiar with: patriarchy, war, monotheism, capitalism,
industrialization, etc. I don't foresee us going back to caves or anything
particularly primal, nor do I see the end of conflict per se, but what I do
see is that all the current systems that have maintained us and driven our
reality and paradigms are failing badly. They simply won't survive and will
take down those who refuse to shift gears and surf the changes with open
architecture and open hearts. Facilitating the emergent shift will be
networks of like-minded folks who engage in mutual aid, education and
support, alternative economic and media practices, nonviolent communication
(and resistance), some kind of meditation or yogic practice, a little punk
rebellion, and dynamic integration between local production for food and
living, and global exchange for culture and art. I also know that it will be
like nothing we expect, and at the same time quite normal. It's the ancient
future. Incidentally, everything I mentioned is already happening-- so enjoy
the ride!
