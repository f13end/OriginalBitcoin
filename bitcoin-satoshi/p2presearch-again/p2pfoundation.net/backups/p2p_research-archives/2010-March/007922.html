Dear Michel,

finally, here it is:

A Copyright Exception for Monetizing File-Sharing
A proposal for balancing user freedom and author remuneration in the
Brazilian copyright law reform
http://www.vgrass.de/?p=193

For a bit of background, on why the B2B flat-rates are no alternative and on
why received intuitions fail us with respect to p2p file-sharing:
http://www.ip-watch.org/weblog/2009/05/11/the-world-is-going-flat-rate/

And an even older text:
State of the Culture Flat-Rate
http://waste.informatik.hu-berlin.de/Grassmuck/Texts/08-03_state-of-flatrate.html

I realized that most other text I had wanted to send you are in German. If
you are interested anyway please let me know. But the new text has all the
essential arguments and the most recent materials, so I think that's the
best to work with.

I think you will also be interested in the Benkler et al. text referred to
in one of the footnotes on voluntary payment systems that will come out as a
Berkman Center Working Paper any day now. Was a real eye opener (iOpener?)
for me.

Please ping me when your text is published.

Thanks & best,
Volker
