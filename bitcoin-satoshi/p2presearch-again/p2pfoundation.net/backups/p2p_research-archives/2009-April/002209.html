I suspect that it links to the discussion on the threat of networks...it's a
set of chic neo-ludditism by elites afraid they will be made redundant.
This time it isn't a guy with a saw or a wrench, it's a designer, an
architect, a doctor or accountant being threatened by machines and by p2p
networks that swarm data into self-improving algorithms.

One of the responses will be radical localism and agrarian luddite / hippy
revolt.  But as so many science fiction writers have suggested, I think the
real sway will be toward micro-tech innovations--garage shops of
super-computer driven biotechnology and engineering.  The future belongs to
the tinkerer.  It always has.

Ryan Lanham
