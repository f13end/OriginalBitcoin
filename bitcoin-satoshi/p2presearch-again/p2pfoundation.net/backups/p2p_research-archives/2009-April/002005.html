> In my mind it's never about growth per se but about continuous flows... The
> river flows because of accumulation and melting of snow on the mountain top
> (or underground water accumulation from rain) and that's part of a system of
> flows involving heat, water, vapor, and wind (and probably others)
>
> The point is as long as there is a continuous flow of energy in the system
> then you can have prosperity (assuming fair distribution of resources etc)
> ... but at some point you reach a capacity limit (or logistic threshold) and
> you need to either grow the pond or shrink the population.. I vote for
> growing the pond!
>
