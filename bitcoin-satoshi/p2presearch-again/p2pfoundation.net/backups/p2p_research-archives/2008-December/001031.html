*Reality Sandwich's Featured Contributor: ST
Frequency<http://www.realitysandwich.com/sites/realitysandwich.civicactions.net/modules/civicrm/extern/url.php?u=2285&qid=73862>
*

ST Frequency is the alias most associated with Atlanta-based writer and
musician Stephen Thomas. As part of the artist collective Kids with
Codenames, ST has organized electronic music events and has self-released
several eclectic EPs. He holds a BA in English from Georgia State University
and has studied British and American Culture at the University of
Northumbria at Newcastle. ST Frequency is a contributing editor at Reality
Sandwich and an organizer for RS events in Atlanta. His interview with
"Footsteps in Africa" director Kathi von Koerber can be read
*here*<http://www.realitysandwich.com/sites/realitysandwich.civicactions.net/modules/civicrm/extern/url.php?u=2273&qid=73862>.


RS: What brought you to Reality Sandwich?

A. As a longtime fan of Daniel Pinchbeck's work, I was a regular visitor to
the forums at BreakingOpentheHead.com. I got that "knowing" feeling when I
saw Daniel's request for contributors for a new web magazine, and quickly
got to work on a few demo pieces, which became among the first to run on
Reality Sandwich! As a contributing editor and, most recently, an organizer
for local RS events in Atlanta, I'm incredibly grateful for the opportunity
to connect with all our readers and to be of service in furthering this
visionary community. It's been truly magical, and the best writing gig I
could imagine.

RS: What transformations do you hope to see in your lifetime?

A. That's a tough question, as I'm daily wondering exactly what the near
future has in store for us. But whatever may come, I am certain that our
quirky camp of evolutionaries will blaze the way into the next age. I
encourage all who read this to take an active role in the transitioning --
organizing RS events in your hometown is a great start!
