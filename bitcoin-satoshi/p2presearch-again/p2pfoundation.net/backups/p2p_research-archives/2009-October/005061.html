On Monday 19 October 2009, SIGCHI.be, the Belgian professional association
for Human-Computer Interaction (www.sigchi.be), organises its first full-day
conference on New Communities.
Theme: New Communities

This year's theme addresses the heightened interest in building and
supporting communities in all its shades: groupware, work communities,
social networks, community software & tools. It also refers to the new
community of Belgian HCI professionals that this conference wants to bring
together, enabling people to step forward and present their work to their
peers and to the broader ICT community.
Timing

Monday 19 October 2009, from 8:30 till 18:00

*Program*

  08:30 - 09:00 Arrival 09:00 - 10:00 Welcome speech and opening keynote  10:00
- 10:30 Morning break 10:30 - 12:00 Research paper presentations 12:00 -
13:30 Lunch break 13:30 - 15:30 Case studies 15:30 - 16:00 Afternoon
break 16:00
- 17:00 Research paper presentations 17:00 - 18:00 Closing keynote

*Keynotes*

09:00 - 10:00

*Communities of Practice: A conceptual frame for Human-Centred Computing*

Volker Wulf, University of Siegen and Fraunhofer FIT, Sankt Augustin

 17:00 - 18:00

*Geekiness is the new cool*

Tom Palmaerts, Trendwolves



At the conference, Namahn's Kristel Van Ael  and Joannes Vandermeulen will
present a practical case study on*The animated GUI: lessons from Disney*.

*Location*

De Montil, Moortelstraat 8, 1790 Affligem - www.demontil.be

*Pricing*


   - SIGCHI.be or ACM members: 60 eur
   - Non-SIGCHI.be members: 120 eur
   - Students: 40 eur

*Info and registration*

You can find more info on the conference
page<http://www.sigchi.be/blog/p/detail/2009-fall-conference-on-new-communities>
or
register on the signup page
<http://www.sigchi.be/events/p/signup/2009-fall-conference-new-communities>of
the SIGCHI.be website.

*Target audience*

 Our first conference will bring together researchers and practitioners like
usability specialists, designers, computer scientists and all people with a
heart and mind set for Human-Computer Interaction.

I hope to see you at the conference.
