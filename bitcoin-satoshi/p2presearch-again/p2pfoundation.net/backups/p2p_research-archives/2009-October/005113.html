My definition of work requires a fair amount of argumentation to legitimize.
My mother recently remarried, before that, three years ago, I was kicked out
of the house by her now husband: one reason was he categorized my behavior
as 'bum', but that as I know was a front, as he heard only complaint from my
mother, and wanted to ensure the safety of his younger daughters, by
insisting I do work such as 'cutting fence line' he knew I would refuse. If
I were to say, I work at 'so and so', no argument to justify my work is
required. If I were to say I am a college student, no further justification
is required. To say I am an independent researcher requires a fair amount
of justification within this "culture;" and it has taken a fair amount of
time to develop what sounds acceptable here. If I am asked, 'do you get
paid?', I respond, 'yes'. The payment is not monetary, but it qualifies
based on my definition as payment, and saves further inquiry from someone I
probably do not want to know well anyway.

So I would say "work" is what activity valued in life and will depend based
on your personality and environment; nurture vs. nature; what the individual
within individualist culture (today often mistaken as "nature") is important
or founded on that of the community when the culture values the external
moreso. What I described was based on a perhaps: a p2p bias. Dante, might
'nurture/nature' be an umbrella "spectrum" to add to your model? And so too
with it, 'individualism' and 'community'. Here are some others you may want
to adopt/revise, which basically represent variations upon the same or
similar theme:

Law enforcement in the form of a human career is a sign of the weakness of
narrow linear models produced by a world-view based on competition first,
collaboration second, if at all: propriety = lack of knowledge =
protectionism = prison. The person within a market culture live as linear
minds; reduction before induction; what's in it for me before I give a damn
about you; and so on. The p2p model is a reversal of sorts, nurturing a
cyclical mentality, where the most power is noted as produced from the
external world, and it is addressed first before reflecting on the internal
self that surfaces after. This may be what Paul Fernhout means by "maturing
as a society."

Once universal peer production is established by the socio-technical means
Sam, Dante, and I are developing with you (whether you know it or not), how
we ascert value in such an environment will differ somewhat from the
regulated marketplace that has brought us this far; but we will still have
values, a sense of right and wrong, even if those sensibilities are enhanced
in a culture where difference is more than acceptable, not like before where
the market society had difficulty in caring for themselves, resorting to
protective means of intellectual and physical enclosure for self
preservation within the reductive expanse.

I quickly grow tired of thinking; of writing; it is useful for a time; but I
do it more than what is healthy; but such is needed in these times; we must;
yet I'd much rather be the abstractions they themselves reflect upon.
