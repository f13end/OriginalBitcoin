These are just hypotheses of mine:

regular wikipedia contributors are wonderful people... that have
freely decided to waste huge amounts of their own time fighting
against trolls that continuously rewrite andvandalize pages for the
fun of it, just because everybody must have write access. And endure
heartburn from that and many other causes. It continuously amazes
me. So I guess that this specific feature, if really is so heavily
used, is the last of their problems.

maybe it was included in the software simply because in the very first
days there were so little users, all good guys and knowing each other,
that nobody thought seriously about future issues like scalability,
trolls, astroturfing and the like. Just like it happened to email,
which eventually led to spam.
