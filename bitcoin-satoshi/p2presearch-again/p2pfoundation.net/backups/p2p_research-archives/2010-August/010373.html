> It's worth thinking about how Digg compares to the
> http://newstrust.net/ community. The two sites arose from different
> cultures, and very different understandings of media ecologies.
>
> Digg, in part, comes from the begginings of the Web 2.0 bubble,
> Silicon Valley, and the notion of "crowdsourcing". Content over
> people. I imagine the majority of Digg users as people in office
> cubicles, who are not really supposed to even be using the internet
> while at work, and who sneak quick peeks and comments and submissions.
> It's like a snack, or junkfood news.
>
> Newstrust comes more from the perspective of "Virtual Community",
> still in part origins from Silicon Valley, but a different culture
> that leans more towards people over content. I think the quality of
> Newstrust voluntary contribution is higher, and I think it is because
> it is more people centered.
>
