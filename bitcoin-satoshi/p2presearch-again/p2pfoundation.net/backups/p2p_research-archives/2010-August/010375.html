> I don't have time right now to get into this to comment, but from reading

> social news selection systems).
>
> Reminds me very much of Wikipedia, which apparently has been hijacked
> (gamed), at least for some important parts of the knowledge commons, by
> groups of editors backing each other up and easily able to suppress the
> expression of viewpoints that are thought wrong or unwanted by the
> group/groups that employ/motivate those editors.
>
> Google is another example. Ad space providers try to game the system to
> increase their traffic (and ad revenue) over and above what would be normal.
> Large advertisers, on  the other hand, lean on Google to game its own system
> to bring their sites to the top of the search results pages, while sending
> the pages of critics further down where they are unlikely to be found by the
> average search engine user.
>
> How to prevent this from happening ... I wouldn't have a clue.
