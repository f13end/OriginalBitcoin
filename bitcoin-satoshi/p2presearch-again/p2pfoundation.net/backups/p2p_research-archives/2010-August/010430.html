On Tue, Aug 24, 2010 at 3:52 PM, Jared Hardy <jaredhardy at dataroads.org>wrote:

> Dear Michel Bauwens,
>    I want to introduce you to a new mesh network routing concept, to
> both supersede and enhance the ICANN-dependent Internet:
>
> http://DataRoads.Org/ <http://dataroads.org/>
>
> The idea is to create an open source router software stack and
> proof-of-concept open hardware, that enable a neighbor-to-neighbor
> network physical topology. Geoposition numbering acts to replace
> ICANN-imposed IP numbering, and user-centric relative naming replaces
> or enhances ICANN domain-hierarchy naming. This would act to
> strengthen peer-to-peer logical topology communications, and bring in
> participants who are disenfranchised from the current ICANN-dependent
> system. Please let me know what you think, especially if you would be
> interested in serving as a foundational board member, or volunteering
> your vast mental reserves in some other way.
>
>    Thank you,
>    Jared Hardy
