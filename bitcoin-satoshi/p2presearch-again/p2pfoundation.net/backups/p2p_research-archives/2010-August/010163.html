> I'm not so sure that we can actually link Universal Design (as it is called
> in the USA) or Design for All (as it is called in Europe) to P2P trends.
> I studied it about 10 years ago but I remember it's not about participatory
> design or design for enabling p2p dynamics or open models. Though it is very
> important, it is about designing products, services and so on in a way that
> they are proper for disabled people and "common people" at the same time. No
> more "special" products for disabled people and "common" products for
> everybody else: Universal Design proposes to design all the products as if
> they were to be used by everybody (hence the "universal" name).
>
> It's about considering ergonomics (but not participation) in the design
> process. I don't see it as P2P, it is a way to design better products for
> everybody.
>
