> The easiest example (but there are surely others) of full time
> professionals to not touch is doctors. Would you trust somebody who
> grabs a surgical blade only once every six weeks because he or she has
> to self produce his own bricks, food, clothes... to slice your belly
> or fill a cavity?



This is a good example, because you can't actually trust doctors nor the
mainstream medical system without a much stronger 'p2p' component.

- the medical system is beholden to prive for-profit interests that for
example result in an enormous amount of false research, in oversubscribing
detrimental medicines etc..

- communities of patients learn a lot from each other and create their own
knowledge base, such as the AIDS communities have done ...

this is happening on a grand scale and challenging the expert-only model of
medicine: http://delicious.com/mbauwens/P2P-Healthcare
