Hi David,

I indeed think production is one of the core variables, because it concerns
the reproduction  of our species, but I see this 'integratively', as
proceeding from many different factors, including the dominant value sets.

As for the state, I think it has a triple function, which must co-exist,
i.e. it reflects the dominant power, it reflects the interests of its own
personnel, and because of the need for legitmacy, must also reflect the
balance of social power.

However, because social innovation (peer production, co-design, co-creation,
etc...) is now a absolute condition for competitivity and survival, it's
also in its own interest to 'enable and empower social participation', which
I think will be the basis of the new social contract,

Michel
