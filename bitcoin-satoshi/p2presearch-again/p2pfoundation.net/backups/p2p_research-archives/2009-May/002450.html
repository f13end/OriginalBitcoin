City Planning, Civic Engagement and the Internet
http://ccisummit.princeton.edu/index.html

A three day summit exploring the Web's potential to create better urban
places.
Thursday April 30 - Saturday 2 May 2009

Dr Mark Elliott (Collabforge) will be presenting the Opening Plenary:
Using Web Tools For Collaborative Planning
See how web tools are being used to engage communities in planning
processes.
Friend Center Convocation Room

With most of the world’s population now living in cities and concern
mounting about the economic, social and environmental challenges the face,
interest in the field of urban planning has risen tremendously.

To meet some of these challenges, planners, policymakers, technology
specialists, and citizen advocates are working to leverage the immense
potential of the Internet to rethink how people can participate in decisions
about the shape and direction of their community.

    * Learn best practices for increasing public participation using the
Internet.
    * Find out how governments, non-profit organizations, and neighborhood
groups are gatering and providing data online.
    * See some of the latest web tools designed to help residents find out
about trends and changes in their neighborhood.

Attendence is free, however space is limited and registration is required.
