Hi Michel,

Convictions are strong, non-changing beliefs in some ideals...
Principles are convictions set in stone.

So it's just a matter of the degree of rigidity of our belief that we
go from ideals to convictions to principles.

I explained in my previous message to Ryan that I'm in it for the
Creative Destruction of what is, so it makes sense for me to widen my
base of common beliefs by going with the least rigid form of belief,
the idea of the ideal.

But I see that P2P Foundation has a mission to change the world; not
simply engage in the creative destruction of what is, but to define
what can be and work to make it happen (as oppose to define it as a
possibility to give people something to think about...)

I think that's the key and main difference in the way we're going
about it and you certainly have done a lot more than I have (and will
be true for many years and potentially decades or for all my
existence) to awaken people to the possibility of something different,
but whereas my goal ends right there your goal is to actually bring
about the 'possibility' that you've worked hard to envision, define
and promote.

By the way, had I realized this about my own way I would not have
argued so vigorously for it, and it would have been better to act as
an agitator of imagination than an agitator of egos. However,
creativity does not flow without emotional heat, and the emotional
tension that is its precursor, so a little ego agitation is not too
bad, but I could have been a little more focused on agitating the
imagination (of myself and others) and less on agitating the
underlying emotions.

The Stefan episode definitely helped me reach this awareness...
because of how stark it is as an example of being defined by ideology.

Marc
