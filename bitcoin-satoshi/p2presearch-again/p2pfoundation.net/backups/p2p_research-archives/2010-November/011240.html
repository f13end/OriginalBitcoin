> The Aschero´s Musical System (Numberphony) can be compared in the
> field of mathematics to the pass from Roman to Arabic numeral. As
> Aschero points out “writing in a treble clef in XXth century is
> similar to draw a G in gothic style”. Therefore, if today nobody took
> it into his head, there is not reason for keeping writing on staff.
> Especially if we are aware of the failures of previous methods getting
> successful results.
> writing, playing and composing.
> Aschero´s Numberphony is a revolutionary replacement for the
> traditional music notation system making use of simple, universal
> symbols (e.g. numbers, shapes and colors).
> Simplifies and expedites the musical learning process.
> And is the most effective and rational way of representing all the
> existing sounds.
> Aschero´s Numberphony is the result of 35 years of extensive research
> and hard work.
> The system has been extensively tested, approved and recognized by
> several music academies, well-know experts and governmental
> institutions in Europe, America and Asia.
> The system is currently being used by some of institutions in Spain
> and Italy as a music learning method. Sergio Aschero, the author of
> Aschero´s Numberphony, is an Argentine scientist and researcher who
> has dedicated his life to finding new methods for teaching music.
> He studied in Argentina, Spain and Italy, earning a Master of
> Musicology at University Complutense of Madrid.
