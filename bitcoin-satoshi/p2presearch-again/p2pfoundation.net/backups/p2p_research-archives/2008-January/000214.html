Michel,

It's an interesting concept, for sure. I was workignfor a while on a social
software project that included the creator of Grazr, and we were integrating
a custom version of Grazr as the main interface of the tool-suite.

One of the interesting things about tools like Grazr, is that although Garzr
themselves will eventually charge money for some parts of the service
http://grazr.com/info/faq.html, or sell it off etc, tools like Grazr can be
fairly easily developed by extending firefox
http://developer.mozilla.org/en/docs/Extensions and maybe Flock
http://developer.flock.com/wiki/Main_Page

There are actually quite a few tools and concepts I've seen people talking
about over the past year (like visualizing data, etc) that could go
client-side like this, and possibly work much better than server based tools
and code.
