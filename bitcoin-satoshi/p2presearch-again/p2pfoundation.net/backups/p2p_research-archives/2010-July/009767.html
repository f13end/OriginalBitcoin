On Tue, Jul 20, 2010 at 5:54 PM, Alex Rollin <alex.rollin at gmail.com> wrote:

> Hello everyone, and especially wiki editors.
>
> The last years have been tough on Mediawiki.  As some of you might know the
> software is built to force collaboration and makes the exclusion of editors
> difficult  when it comes to some forms of "graded" participation.  Because
> the platform is so widely used the motivation to created automated spam
> routines is high, and plagues every group using the software.
>
> Our solution for now is that anyone can create a login, and when they
> confirm their email they can do a bit more.  New (and old) users are part of
> this class, and are forced to fill out the forms that test for humanity.
>
> Spammers get through still, though.  Yesterday I blocked an account for
> posting product links on our super popular product hacking page.  Took
> several page views and some time.
>
> This is made easier with more people patrolling the wiki!
>
> So, I would like to invite those of you with account to go ahead and write
> your name on the following page:
>
> http://p2pfoundation.net/Help:Become_A_Confirmed_Wiki_Editor
>
> I'd like to promote any of you real human editors out there who do work on
> the wiki.  Any of the admins can do the promotion, and this can be a page
> where we define the rights of the "class of users."  Feel free to use the
> talk page to discuss.
>
