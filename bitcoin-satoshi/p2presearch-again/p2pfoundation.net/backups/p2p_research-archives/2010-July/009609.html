different folks, different strokes,

again to repeat as this was a concrete case:

1) the transclusion in policy referred to 3 same links in one and a half
line

2) something got wrong in the subhierarchy so that subsection that belonged
to a former subsection turned out in a next unrelated section

3) the title was inconsistent with 2 other titles in the same subheading
category, etc...

so, logically, I wanted to change it, but in the case of transclusion, 1) I
have to check the manual 2) make sure changes are compatible in different
sections with different subheading hierarchies ... in other words, I have to
spent 30 minutes instead of 30 seconds, with no guarantee of success ...
some people like to do this, I have other fish to fry, again, different
folks, different strokes, don't impose unitary solutions to diverse people
and diverse page and category contexts ..

it is true that categories could be much improved and that the large amount
of content makes it difficult to digest,

but one-size fits all transclusion doesn't even begin to address this issue
... it's not related ...

a much more elegant solution was devised by our good friend nathan, i.e.
double columns, a simple solution that gives a much easier overview look ...

there might be other solutions, but it's not transclusion ... I'm open to
discussion solutions with you and other people as that would really improve
user-friendliness
