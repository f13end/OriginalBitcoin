I don't necessarily think it clouds the issue to adhere to some basic
principles:

- do not edit the wiki in such a way that you impede the freedom of others

- do  not create additional technical barriers to participation and do not
require participants to read manuals in order to be able to cooperate

- honour the distinctions between code and text, and the nuances between
different concepts

- in case someone has problems with some problematic edits, do not tell them
to read the f ..ing manual, but be friendly and responsive

In this particular context, I have no problems whatsoever with the creation
of a second layer of the wiki with positivist and experimental content, BUT
I do not want this new layer to expel and replace the four years of work
that have gone in the creation of the first empirical/emergent and
conceptual layer ...

When I point out some issues when both layers are in conflict, because the
second intends to take away the freedom to continue to develop the first,  I
do not appreciate defensive and dismissive responses that amount to a "go
f.. yourself, I'm the boss now". I want a little respect for the elders who
have done an enormous amount of work to make this happen, and do not
appreciate an authoritarian barging in ..
