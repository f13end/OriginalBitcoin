Thanks for the replies on the subject - all very interesting.

I think that while evolutionary psychology has a way to do, it is not 
sufficient to dismiss either it ideas or larger narrative on the basis 
that it contradicts our own bias of what we'd like to be true.  There is 
an interesting audio of Pinker defending the position of evolutionary 
psychology here: 
http://www.pointofinquiry.org/steven_pinker_evolutionary_psychology_and_human_nature/

Since my days doing psychology and linguistics in university, I was 
persuaded by the arguments of Chomsky and the rest that humans were 
pre-wired for language.  On that basis it seems to me that we will also 
be pre-wired for a number of other things - social relations included.
