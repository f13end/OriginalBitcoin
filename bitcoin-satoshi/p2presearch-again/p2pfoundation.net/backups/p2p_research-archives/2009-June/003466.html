Hi Christian,

I think that Ryan uses materialism in the 'conventional' sense, and there
anti-materialist, in the sense of "not focused on material things" but on
other, more ethical and ideal aspects of life,

while you use it in the classic philosophical sense, which really has the
opposite meaning, materialism meaning, 'rooted in the material world'

In this sense, Marxism sees phenomena as rooted in the material world, but
is anti-materialist in the sense that life is not about accumulating
material goods ...

Michel
