> I am uploading to Dropbox about 900 images from books I’ve scanned and from
> the Web, representing the conformations of the RMs.  E.g., snuggling or
> sharing food, big statues of dictators and gods, randomization devices for
> EM, diverse forms of money, etc.  They include images from many cultures and
> many historical and prehistoric periods, organized in folders corresponding
> to the RM and the sub-type of conformation. There are also images of
> homologous or analogous behaviors in other species.
>
> I’m putting them up in case you want to use them in research, teaching,
> lectures, etc.  You can certainly pass them on to other scholars and
> students, too, but most of them are copyrighted, so please be moderately
> circumspect in sharing the link and the images.  Information about the
> images and their sources is usually available by looking at the file
> properties.  (I haven’t figured out whether you can view document properties
> of files in a ‘public’ Dropbox folder, but you can see them if you download
> the file and right click it in Windows.)  For better or worse, many of them
> are large, high-resolution files.
