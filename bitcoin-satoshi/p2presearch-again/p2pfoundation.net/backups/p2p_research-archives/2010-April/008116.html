attached and below is the text of the *Copyright 4 Creativity *declaration;
an *FAQ* about the campaign and about signing on; and a set of mainstream
*examples of exceptions to & limitations* on copyright. If you're interested
in *signing on*, please contact Nick Ashton-Hart at
nashton at copyright4creativity.eu <mailto:nashton at copyright4creativity.eu>,
cc:ing Joe & me. There will also be a *conference call tomorrow* where new
signers and interested parties can ask questions about the campaign.

Though summarized in greater detail in the several documents, I wanted to
highlight a major aspect of the campaign as an articulation of the positive
agenda - what we're for in this debate. This is in contrast to the IPR
Enforcement agenda (ACTA, IPRED2, 3 Strikes), but more importantly an
explanation of why flexibility in copyright is more conducive to creativity
and innovation and the growth of the digital economy. It is also an effort
to promote the Copyright for the Knowledge Economy dossier that the European
Commission began last year. In connection with the work plan for exceptions
& limitations at the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO) and the
currently discussed Treaty for the Visually Impaired, this effort is crucial
since the strongest opposition to these developments at WIPO has been the EU
delegation.

I also wanted to highlight the collection of examples that resonate with
people about the ways in which copyright exceptions & limitations impact
people's lives and their personal, social, and entrepreneurial activities.
New signatories are invited (& very much encouraged) to contribute one or
more examples to the list that will be available on the campaign website.

Finally, this project and campaign is about broadening the communities and
advocacy circles connected to and working on these copyright issues. You
will notice in the list of signatories library groups, trade associations,
and music industry representation. One challenge of the campaign will be to
try and bring in a diverse set of signatories representing a broad range of
constituencies.

Please consider having your organization sign on to the Copyright 4
Creativity Declaration to advance the positive agenda on copyright in the
EU. Please also let your contacts at organizations peripherally interested
in digital rights and representing kindred constituencies about the
campaign.
