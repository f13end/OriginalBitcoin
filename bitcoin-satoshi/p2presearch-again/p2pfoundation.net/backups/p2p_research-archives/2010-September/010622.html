>> Cooperative practices and values that challenge the status quo while, at
>> the same time, creating alternative modes of economic, cultural, social, and
>> political life have emerged with dynamism in recent years. The 15 articles
>> in this issue--written by activists, coop practitioners, theorists,
>> historians, and researchers--begin to make visible some of the myriad modes
>> of cooperation existing today around the world that both directly respond to
>> new enclosures and crises *and* show pathways beyond them. Prefiguring
>> other possibilities for organizing life and provisioning for our needs and
>> desires, we call these cooperative experiments *the new cooperativism*.
