Thanks for your response.  My own view is that violence outside of state
structures or immediate self defense is nearly always wrong.  There are, I
suppose, just revolutions against tyranny of one sort or another.  My own
view is that these should be few if there is an open society, free
elections, an open economy (or relatively so) and the rule of law.  One can
be too quick to see tyranny and abuse in a species that is, after all, only
a few thousand generations removed from living mostly in caves and tree
tops.  32,000 years ago, we were biologically essentially the same as now,
but we had little or no concept of anything we call governance, fairness,
justice or most other fineries of civilization we now rely on.  Or so the
best experts seem to think.

I may be tainted in some minds for adopting the mores of conventional
society, and in finding comfort in stable governments and economies, and you
are right in that I believe that thinking that way is in my own best
interest, the interests of those I care most about, and the interests of
most people I know.

Revolution may sweep me and my type aside.  If it does, so be it.  We are a
vicious species.  I will defend myself as any other sane person or culture
would.  My guess is that the new boss will be the same as the old boss...or
worse.  And we will get fooled again.
