Thanks Tere and Athina, I am so pleased with your input, comments and clarifications. I have been quite busy preparing for a consultancy gig from tomorrow morning so am up to my ears. So thanks for picking up on these things with the group. 

 

It is indeed the case that one can publish in more than one place, as long as publishers are in agreement and copyrights are managed appropriately. Yes, academic publishing doesn't follow the same principles as GPL obviously, but it is always flexible, and repeat publications have been incorporated into the RAE as well.

 

I spent all of last Friday putting together a description of what I intend to do,  and incorporating all abstracts I'd been given so far, and then presented this to the Editorial Board of Capital and Class on Saturday (most of whom have never heard of p2p so took some convincing, but in the end, I got it passed. Steve Fleetwood is now on the board and he is pretty excited about the project as well). 

 

As this will be a special issue, I individually will have the final word on what is included, so editorial awkwardness can hopefully be circumvented. The journal is very highly esteemed and accepts fewer articles than it rejects-also please note that it is second only to New Left Review in left wing journals on the nationally recognised citation index. So we are in a 'win win' situation.

 

I will send the proposal I've managed to put together early next week because now, as I said, I'm off to the Balkans in the morning and back Friday.

 

Hope you are all well.

Hi again,

I agree with Tere, I dont see a onflict of interest here. As far as I am aware, one can publish in a journal and then with the journal's permission (which is always given), publish the same or similar piece in a book or an edited volume. It has happened a lot especially with work in progress. In fact, in british academic terms, the last Research Assessment Exercise, the same/similar piece of work, if published by two different venues could count twice (this was not so in the RAE before that). 

This is technical stuff, my point really is that some people's work might be suitable for C&C (if it goes through as its editors can be difficult), and others an edited volume somewhere, some can do both, they do not exclude each other (its allowed!) and much more so, since work progresses, and by the time a book comes up, material will be expanded and better to publish anyway.
