Thanks Paul for pointing out the absurdity of this article and for
developing knowledge tools to plot and scale abundance. ;) The words that
follow I hope nourishes further insight into these regards.

The 60 jobs draft assumes continued centralization and specialization all of
which can only be secured by general public distrust. Trust must be secured
through technological mechanisms to scale large populations. We're already
seeing this trust spread as folk come online to share their personal
ambitions.
We will see, as I am seeing more with each day, that we were afraid of a
phantom. In so doing, this phantom was given life as a legitimate fear, but
only because knowledge was scarce and mismanaged for central gain with
neighbors silent to secure these centralisms or specializations to subsist
in the highly competitive platform these fears and uncertainties created.

Answer: Automated adaptive knowledge representations and access to
materials. Trust and the fruits that come of it in such a world are
'built-in'.
