This is the attempted Schedule of the Forum from simona conservas

First day: October 29th
Oxcars. The greatest free culture event of all times: A public annual event
to standardize in mainstream format the cultural practices under the
collaborative culture paradigm. http://exgae.net/los-Oxcars

Second day: October 30th
Open Conferences about the chapters we will be working on during the next
days. The aim of this conferences will be framing the issues we will be
dealing with. Given that the third day will be devoted to a programmatic
work all the ideological and conceptual queries will be debated this second
day.

9.30 Opening and accreditation

Education 10- 11.30 pm
Carlos Castro
Anne Ostergaard
Students for free Culture
Bonaventura dos Santos Sousa (to be confirmed)

11.30- 13 Policy
David Bollier
Manuel Castells (to be confirmed)
Jamie King
Joanne richardson (to be confirmed)
Felix Stalder

Lunch and Meeting Point 13 - 15

15-16.45 Hackers
Richard Stallman (video Conference)
Chaos Computer (to be confirmed)
Pirate Byran+ Pirate Bay
Nagarjuna G. (video conference)

16.45-18.30 Economy
Jonh Howkins
Michel Bauwens
Koleman Strumpf (video conference)
Jeremy Rifkin (video conference)
Carlos Formenti


-18.30-19.00 Coffee Break

19.00-21.00 Legal and users
Consumers International
Lawrence Lessig (to be confirmed)
EDRI
EFF
Center For Internet and society
Pirate Party

Third day: October 31st
We will be divided in working groups to elaborate specific reivindications.
Each working group will start the debate from documents we will have already
being working on.

Fourth day: November 1st
10.00-13.00
Plenary with all the groups to compare conclusions and unify claims in a
final document.
15.00 17.00 Writing of the Common document
17.00-21  Networking organizations and diffusion strategies.

We also want to introduce the website of the Forum, www.fcforum.net where we
will be adding information.
