Hi Ryan,

communalism is not an academic journal, but a movement journal ...

undoubtedly, the way research is vetted will change, as will the forms in
which it is distributed ...

I haven't followed the field close enough but there are different attempts
to go beyond this and peer review and I agree this is a theme we should
tackle in the future ...

this being said we need different media for ultrashort message, long books
and everything in between; changing institutional habits takes time however
...

you will have noticed that people associated with oekonux and the p2p
foundation still seem enthusiastic to start their own journal, I guess it's
part of the process of social acceptance ...

Michel

>> *Communalism* announce:
>> New journal will be presented in December

>> As many of you have noticed already, we are planning a major update of
>> this journal. Now we are proud to announce what these changes will come in
>> December this year.
>>
>> Social ecology needs an international journal that is relevant to
>> activists and sympathizers, and we seek to provide this. From this December,
>> we will start publishing 3 issues per year of the journal. Each issue will
>> consist of several theoretical essays as well as shorter political articles,
>> and we hope it can be used to support your own organizational efforts. In
>> addition to start producing a printed journal, *Communalism* will also
>> start a publishing house for books and pamphlets, and we will make a new and
>> more accessible website. This will all be an English-language project,
>> directed towards an international audience.
>>
>> More information about the development of this project will be sent out to
>> you as soon as possible. If you do not wish to receive further information
>> from *Communalism*, please unsubscribe this newsletter (information

>> develop this project, we look forward to hearing from you. We need
